# Guided-Project-Building-a-BI-App

The goal of this project was to build a Business Intelligence (BI) application in Power BI to monitor quantitative quality data, identify critical pain points, and recommend, based on concrete data, which 5 courses should be prioritized for improvement. 

This guided project was completed on the Dataquest platform, and the project steps are included in the PDF file. I didn’t encounter any major difficulties during the project, as I was always guided by the platform, and I find Power BI to be quite intuitive.

Three data tables were used. After the data model was created, it was possible to perform the analysis and build dashboards.

The BI analysis developed in this application enabled us to transform progress data and NPS feedback into strategic business decisions. As we can see in the “Lesson completion rate” dashboard, using DAX formulas, we calculated an overall completion rate of 76.5% and identified an excellent historical recovery since mid-2021. However, the scatter plot reveals a critical point of friction: an extremely popular course with nearly 50,000 enrollments has a completion rate of only 50%. This deviation, combined with the histogram highlighting courses with near-zero retention, pinpoints the priority areas for improvement to maximize success for all students on the platform.

In the optional challenge of creating a histogram, a huge bubble stands out in the critical zone: moderate NPS (~60) but with a completion rate of only 40%, representing a high volume of frustrated students. Identifying and correcting this specific course will have the greatest impact on the platform’s overall retention.  

In the final dashboard “Dataquest NPS Report,” we see an overall NPS of 62.8 and a high volume of responses with a score of 10; overall satisfaction is strong. However, the timeline reveals significant historical volatility, with a sharp drop in NPS to -28.6 before recovering. The scatter plot on a logarithmic scale highlights major concerns: courses with a significant volume of feedback are recording negative scores, close to -50 and -100. These problematic courses are generating strong dissatisfaction and must be redesigned as a priority to stem the loss of students and protect the platform’s overall reputation.

Finally, a “Recommendation” dashboard was created to identify the courses that need to be reviewed.
